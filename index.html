I am a graduate student at Cornell with interests in machine learning, social network science and datacenter computing. My current ongoing projects are object detection on an embedded system, community detection and machine learning for garbage collection in datacenters. Apart from these projects I have taken courses in machine learning, computer vision, parallel computing and datacenter computing.

During my undergrad in Mumbai, I worked on many embedded systems projects for which I won a couple of awards. In my junior year I became interested in machine learning and started taking online courses on the same. In my final year, I worked on project called 'Deep Learning for Video Compression' which resulted in a paper publication in the IEEE at a conference in IIT Bombay in 2019.

Currently in my Master's program at Cornell, I am working with some of the brightest minds in the field of machine learning, network science and datacenter computing and working on projects under their expertise is an amazing learning experience. In my current semester (Spring'20) I am TA for the course 'Digital Logic and Computer Organization'. Being a TA allows me to refresh my knowlege of the digital system and microprocessors and also, allows me to work on FPGAs which are a bit alien to me. More information about my projects is available under the 'Projects' tab.

Apart from academics I have keen interests in football (soccer) and I have also played table tennis as a professional in the Mumbai district. At Cornell I had the oppurtuinity to learn tennis(PE Fall'19), Jeet Kune Do and MMA (PE Spring'20). I plan to learn many more sports along the way. I also like reading novels and my favorite novel is The Selfish Gene by Prof. Richard Dawkins.
